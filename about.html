New Zealand's famous attractions

Discover the breathtaking landscapes of New Zealand - our sacred duty to protect and preserve the natural world for future generations.

Milford Sound

Fiordland, South Island

A stunning fjord surrounded by towering peaks, cascading waterfalls, and pristine rainforest. Known as the "eighth wonder of the world," this UNESCO World Heritage site exemplifies the raw beauty we must protect through Kaitiakitanga.

Hobbiton Movie Set

Matamata, North Island

The famous movie set from The Lord of the Rings and The Hobbit trilogies, showcasing New Zealand's rolling green hills. This attraction demonstrates how film tourism can coexist with environmental preservation and sustainable land use.

Rotorua Geothermal Region

Bay of Plenty, North Island

A unique geothermal wonderland featuring hot springs, geysers, and volcanic lakes. This region shows how indigenous Māori culture and natural phenomena can coexist harmoniously while maintaining environmental balance.

Aoraki/Mount Cook

Canterbury, South Island

New Zealand's highest peak, sacred to Māori as the ancestor frozen in stone. The mountain and surrounding national park showcase alpine ecosystems vital to our environmental heritage and climate research.

Tongariro National Park

Central North Island

New Zealand's first national park and a UNESCO World Heritage site, featuring active volcanoes, alpine lakes, and diverse ecosystems. This sacred Māori landscape demonstrates the perfect integration of cultural heritage and environmental conservation through traditional Kaitiakitanga practices.

Lake Tekapo

Canterbury, South Island

A stunning turquoise alpine lake surrounded by lupins and offering spectacular mountain views. This area represents the importance of protecting New Zealand's freshwater ecosystems and maintaining water quality.
